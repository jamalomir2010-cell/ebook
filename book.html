Chapter 2: The Middleman Business Model

Fiverr Arbitrage, also known as service reselling or the freelance middleman method, is a simple online business model.

Here's how it works in plain English:

  1. A client comes to you on Fiverr and pays you for a service (like a logo design for $50).
  2. You take that order and hire another freelancer to do the job for less (for example, $15).
  3. You deliver the finished work back to your client.
  4. You keep the difference as your profit.

That's it.

You don't need to know how to design logos, build websites, or edit videos. You only need to know how to:

  • Communicate clearly with clients.
  • Find reliable freelancers.
  • Manage the process.

Think of it like running a small agency without having to hire employees. You're not selling your skillsâ€”you're selling your ability to connect demand (clients) with supply (freelancers).

The best part? Clients don't care who does the actual work. They just care about the final result. As long as you deliver on time and with quality, you can build a reputation, get positive reviews, and grow your income.

Chapter 7: Handling Clients Professionally

Having a good gig is only the beginning. The real money comes when you know how to manage each order smoothlyâ€”from the moment the client messages you, until you deliver the final product.

Here's the exact workflow I use in my Fiverr Arbitrage business:

  • Be professional, polite, and clear.

Example response:

Fast communication makes you look professional and increases your chances of getting the order.

2. Collect Requirements

Ask the client detailed questions.

For example, if it's a logo design:

  • What is the name of the business?
  • What colors do you prefer?
  • Do you have a style in mind (modern, minimalist, playful)?

The more details you collect, the easier it is to pass the job to your freelancer.

3. Forward the Job to Your Freelancer

Once you have all requirements, send them to your chosen freelancer.

Be clear and organized:

"Client needs a modern logo, blue & white colors, with initials 'AB'. Style: minimalist."

Always set a shorter deadline with your freelancer than the client gave you.

Example: If client expects delivery in 3 days, tell your freelancer to deliver in 2 days.

This gives you buffer time for revisions.

4. Review the Work

When the freelancer sends the finished work, never deliver it to the client immediately.

First, check:

  • Does it match the client's requirements?
  • Is the quality good enough?
  • Are there any mistakes or typos?

If something is wrong, ask your freelancer for revisions.

5. Deliver to the Client

Once everything looks perfect, send it to the client through Fiverr.

Write a friendly delivery message:

"Here's your completed project! I've made sure it meets all your requirements. Please review it and let me know if you'd like any adjustmentsâ€”I'll be happy to make revisions."

6. Handle Revisions

Clients may ask for changes. Don't panicâ€”it's normal.

Simply request the revisions from your freelancer and deliver the updated version.

Always stay professional and polite.

7. Order Completed â†’ You Get Paid

Once the client accepts the order (or after 3 days automatically), Fiverr marks it as complete.

The money will appear in your Fiverr balance (Fiverr keeps 20% as a fee).

You can withdraw your earnings via PayPal, Payoneer, or bank transfer.

With this system, you're simply acting as a bridge between the client and the freelancer. As long as you communicate well and keep everything organized, the process runs smoothly and profitably.
